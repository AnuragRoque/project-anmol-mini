electronic sport

Esports (also known as electronic sports, e-sports, or eSports) is a form of sport competition using video games. Esports often takes the form of organized, multiplayer video game competitions, particularly between professional players, individually or as teams. The most common video game genres associated with esports are multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA), battle royale and real-time strategy (RTS) games. Popular esport franchises include Valorant, League of Legends, Dota 2, Counter-Strike 2, Overwatch and StarCraft, among many others. Although the legitimacy of esports as a true sporting competition remains in question, they have been featured alongside traditional sports in some multinational events in Asia, with the International Olympic Committee also having discussed their inclusion into future Olympic events.
